# Offer Creation Reference

Offer creation frameworks for cold email positioning.

This is a reference file. The SKILL.md orchestrates the interactive walkthrough. Come here for the detailed frameworks, questions, templates, and decision gates.

**Language rule:** The frameworks below use specific terminology (Dream Outcome, Perceived Likelihood, etc.) for structural reference. When talking to the user, translate all of this into simple, everyday language. Think Apple product descriptions: clear, powerful, no jargon. "What you get" not "dream outcome." "How likely it works" not "perceived likelihood." "How fast" not "time delay." "How easy" not "effort and sacrifice." The simpler the words, the more the user trusts the process.

---

## The Value Equation

```
         Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
         Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

| Driver | Goal | What It Means |
| ------ | ---- | ------------- |
| Dream Outcome | INCREASE | The feelings and experiences the prospect envisions. Frame in terms of status gained from the viewpoint of others. |
| Perceived Likelihood | INCREASE | How confident the prospect is this will work for THEM. Driven by proof, specificity, guarantees. |
| Time Delay | DECREASE | Time between buying and receiving benefit. Build in fast wins and early milestones. "Fast beats free." |
| Effort & Sacrifice | DECREASE | Everything the prospect must give up or endure. "Done for you" commands highest prices. |

This is division, not addition. If the bottom approaches zero, value approaches infinity. The best offers focus on reducing the bottom.

## Cold Traffic vs. Warm Traffic Offers

Your main offer that works for referrals and warm leads will probably fail in cold email. Cold traffic needs a different entry point.

The progression that works:
1. **Start with a free, low-barrier offer** that demonstrates your capability without requiring commitment
2. **Make it specific and deliverable** — not "a free consultation" (too generic), but "we'll launch a test campaign and give you any leads it generates"
3. **Test before you build** — don't spend 2 months engineering a lead magnet. Instead of "we have it ready," say "it takes time for me to pull this together, but if I did, would you be the right person to send it to?"

Every offer boils down to one of four B2B angles: **save time, save money, make more money, reduce risk.** Test all four. Your best-performing angle might surprise you.

### The "Niche Down on One Feature" Principle

Don't pitch the whole product or service. Find the one acute pain point that makes ears perk up because it's a known unsolved problem. A CRM with 50 features should lead with the one feature that solves a specific, annoying problem nobody else solves.

For your service: don't lead with "digital marketing." Lead with the specific automation or system that eliminates their most annoying daily task.

## Step 0: Market Validation

### Questions to Ask

1. **"What specific problem does your service solve for this audience?"**
   - Listen for urgency: "we're losing," "we can't," "it's costing us"
   - Red flag: "nice to have" language
   - Probe: "If they don't fix this in 90 days, what happens?"

2. **"Who exactly is buying? Title, company size, industry."**
   - Good: "VP of Sales at Series B SaaS doing $5M to $20M ARR"
   - Bad: "small business owners," "anyone who needs marketing"
   - Probe: "Name 3 actual companies that bought this in the last 6 months"

3. **"Can they afford it? Do they have budget authority?"**
   - Probe: "What's the typical deal size? Who signs the check?"

4. **"Can you build a list of 1,000+ verified emails?"**
   - Probe: "What would you search to find these people?"

5. **"Is this market growing or shrinking?"**

### Decision Gate

| Indicator | Pass | Fail |
| --------- | ---- | ---- |
| Massive Pain | Urgent, costly, keeping them up at night | Nice to have, someday problem |
| Purchasing Power | Decision-maker with budget | Need committee approval, too small |
| Easy to Target | 1,000+ emails buildable | Hard to find, no list path |
| Growing | Market expanding, tailwinds | Shrinking, headwinds |

Three evergreen categories: **Health, Wealth, Relationships.** The niche should sit inside one.

**If any indicator fails, stop and say so.**

## Step 1: Dream Outcome

### Questions

1. "When your service delivers perfectly, what does the client's business look like 90 days later?"
   - Push for specifics: revenue numbers, time saved, problems eliminated
   - Probe: "What would they brag about to their peers?"

2. "Frame that outcome in one sentence with a number and a timeframe."
   - Good: "20 qualified sales meetings in 30 days"
   - Bad: "More leads" or "better marketing"

3. "What status does the buyer gain? How do they look to their boss, board, or peers?"

### Output
A single dream outcome statement: "[Specific result] in [specific timeframe]"

---

## Step 2: Problems and Obstacles

### Questions

1. "What are all the reasons someone would say no to this offer?"
2. "What are all the ways a customer could fail even after buying?"
3. "What does the customer have to DO after they buy? What's hard about that?"

### Organize by Value Equation Drivers

- **Dream Outcome problems:** "What would make them think this won't be financially worth it?"
- **Likelihood problems:** "What would make them doubt this will work for THEM?"
- **Effort problems:** "What would make them think this is too hard?"
- **Time problems:** "What would make them think this takes too long?"

### Output
A numbered list of 15 to 30+ specific problems, categorized by value driver.

---

## Step 3: Solutions List

For each problem from Step 2: "What would completely eliminate this problem?"

Reframe as solutions:
- "It's expensive" becomes "How [service] pays for itself in the first 30 days"
- "I've been burned before" becomes "How to guarantee results so you never waste another dollar"

Don't filter for feasibility yet.

---

## Step 4: Delivery Vehicles

### The Delivery Cube

For each high-value solution:
1. One-on-one, small group, or one-to-many?
2. DIY, DWY (do with you), or DFY (do for you)?
3. What medium? Live calls, email, Slack, video, in-person?
4. Recorded version? Course, video library, playbook?
5. Response time? 24/7, same-day, within 5 minutes?
6. If they paid 10x, what would you add? If 1/10th, what's the minimum?

### Trim (Cost vs Value Matrix)

| | Low Value to Client | High Value to Client |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **High Cost to You** | Remove first | Keep only for premium |
| **Low Cost to You** | Remove second | Keep (sweet spot) |

Focus on one-to-many solutions: templates, playbooks, recorded trainings, tools.

### Output
Trimmed list of delivery vehicles tagged as: core offer, bonus, or cut.

## Step 5: Value Stack & Naming

### Building the Stack

For each surviving component:
1. Give it a branded, benefit-driven name ("Revenue Roadmap Sprint" not "Onboarding Call")
2. Assign a standalone dollar value and justify it
3. Map it to a specific problem from Step 2

| Problem | Solution | Bundle Name | What's Inside | Value |
| ------- | -------- | ----------- | ------------- | ----- |
| [Step 2] | [Step 3] | [branded] | [brief] | $X |

Total value stack should be 3x to 10x the asking price.

### MAGIC Naming Formula

| Letter | Stands For | Purpose |
| ------ | ---------- | ------- |
| M | Magnet | Reason why (Free, 88% off, Grand Opening) |
| A | Avatar | Target announced (Local Restaurant Owners, SaaS Founders) |
| G | Goal | Dream outcome (Double Your Profit, 100k) |
| I | Interval | Timeframe (4 Hour, 21 Day, 90 Day) |
| C | Container | Bundle word (Blueprint, Accelerator, Sprint, System) |

Use 3 to 5 components. Generate 3 to 5 name options.

**Container words:** Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Accelerator, Fast Track, Sprint, Launch, Slingshot, System, Transformation, Mastermind, Workshop, Reset, Solution, Hack, Cheatcode, Liftoff

## Step 6: Enhance the Offer

### Guarantee (Risk Reversal)

Ask: "What is the prospect's biggest fear about paying for this?"
Then: "What would make that fear disappear?"

Formula: **"If you don't achieve [X] in [Y time], we will [specific action]."**

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
| ---- | ------------ | -------- |
| Unconditional | No questions asked refund | Lower ticket, trust-building |
| Conditional | Terms attached, "better than money back" | Higher ticket, serious buyers |
| Performance | Paid on results (per lead, % of revenue) | Cold email specifically |
| Anti-guarantee | "All sales are final" | Proprietary processes |

For cold email: performance models are ideal. Pair with a minimum: "We get the greater of $1,000/mo or 10% of revenue generated."

Stacking: unconditional 30-day + conditional triple-money-back at 90 days.

### Scarcity

Ask: "Is there a real capacity limit?"
- Total business cap: "We only service 25 clients total"
- Growth rate cap: "5 new clients per month"
- Cohort cap: "100 clients 4 times a year"

Only honest scarcity. Fabricated scarcity backfires in cold email.

### Urgency

Ask: "Is there a real reason to act now?"
- Cohort-based: "Next group starts Monday"
- Pricing-based: "This pricing through Friday"
- Exploding opportunity: market inefficiency with a ticking clock

50 to 60% of campaign sales happen in the last 3% of allotted time.

### Bonuses

Ask: "What else could you add that's high value but low cost to deliver?"
Best formats: checklists, tools, swipe files, scripts, templates.

Rules: benefit-driven name, specific dollar value, addresses a Step 2 concern, total bonus value eclipses core price.

## Step 7: Cold Email Validation Checklist

| # | Check |
| - | ----- |
| 1 | Dream Outcome: specific, desirable, framed as status gain? |
| 2 | Perceived Likelihood: proof, specificity, or guarantee? |
| 3 | Time Delay: fast results, immediate wins, compressed timelines? |
| 4 | Effort & Sacrifice: DFY framing, no risk, no heavy lift? |
| 5 | Niche Specificity: prospect thinks "this was made for me"? |
| 6 | Premium Positioning: competing on value, not price? |
| 7 | Category of One: can't be compared to competitors? |
| 8 | Risk Reversal: guarantee or performance model? |
| 9 | Scarcity/Urgency: real reason to act now? |
| 10 | Naming: MAGIC formula? Name signals value? |

If any fails, go back and fix it.

```
[What your service does, framed as DFY/DWY/DIY]

3. [Bundle Name] — [what's inside] — $[value]

[Exact wording with the "or what"]

SCARCITY
[Capacity limit, if real]

URGENCY
[Deadline, if real]

1. [Bonus Name] — [what it is] — $[value]

```

Ask: "Does this feel like something your prospect would be crazy to say no to?"

If yes, proceed to email copy (Phase 4). If no, loop back.

## Pricing Principles

- Never compete on price. There's no benefit to being second cheapest.
- Higher price = higher client investment, better results, better clients, more margin.
- Same deliverable, 100x price difference based on specificity:
  - Generic "Time Management" = $19
  - "Time Management for Outbound B2B Sales Reps" = $1,997

### When Offers Fatigue (Refresh Order)
1. Change the creative
2. Change the body copy
3. Rename the offer (MAGIC rename)
4. Change the duration
5. Change the enhancer
6. Change the price (last resort)
